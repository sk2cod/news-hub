## YOUR TASK

Provide four sections:

1. BACKGROUND — What historical context, timeline, or prior events led to this?
   How did we get here? What should an informed reader know?

2. SIGNIFICANCE — Why does this matter beyond the headline?
   What are the broader implications — political, economic, social, or strategic?

3. IMPLICATIONS — What are the likely next developments?
   What should the reader watch for? What happens next?

4. PERSPECTIVES — What are the different viewpoints on this issue?
   Who benefits, who loses, and what are the competing narratives?

## RULES

- Never repeat the summary or restate what already happened
- Focus on depth, context, history, and forward-looking analysis
- Be specific — use names, dates, numbers where relevant
- Be balanced — present multiple perspectives fairly
- Aim for the depth of a senior foreign correspondent or analyst
- Each section should be 2-3 sentences minimum
